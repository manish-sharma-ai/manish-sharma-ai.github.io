# Premium 9 final audit

## Verdict

The six-cycle autonomous program improved the lowest-credible site score from **7.4** to **8.7**. The unweighted route-matrix average is **8.75**. This is a strong, coherent, technically clean premium system, but the strict 9+ stop condition is **not** claimed.

Two constraints prevent a truthful 9: exact browser-chrome 200% zoom could not be applied in the selected browser harness, and several deliberately text-only informational routes—most clearly `/de/` and `/links/`—remain excellent system pages rather than distinctive 9-level compositions. Solving the second issue without a user-approved asset/content strategy would require inventing imagery, adding decorative filler, or changing content priority, all prohibited by the brief.

## Six autonomous cycles

1. Opened shared narrative/reference visual models, warmed the recovery state, and repaired Demo's off-canvas grid.
2. Replaced Demo's hollow output split, compacted Research into editorial rows, and balanced the mobile footer.
3. Established an evidence hierarchy: open supporting sequences, bounded substantive proof.
4. Unified related routes, provenance, and footer into one quiet ending.
5. Proved and corrected fragile responsive/navigation states, including a measured 3.2px mobile-menu clip.
6. Rebalanced Public Work around one lead proof, supporting cases, open capabilities, and a safe mobile action layout.

Thirteen high-leverage solution hypotheses were compared across shared openings, recovery, Demo, Research, footer, evidence, endings, navigation, responsive menu geometry, Public Work capability/proof layout, and mobile actions. One two-column Public Work mobile CTA candidate was rejected and reworked after it clipped a real label. Two malformed full-page screenshot sets were rejected as evidence because they duplicated tiles. No accepted cycle was retained after a ≥0.3 family regression.
