# Reviewer B — editorial and information design

## Baseline score: 7.5

Headings, labels, and body measures are generally strong. The limiting issue is prioritization: many pages give section introductions, destinations, evidence records, provenance, related paths, and footer groups similar visual weight. This weakens cause-and-effect and makes long mobile pages feel administratively complete rather than editorially paced.

### Rejection evidence

- Research exposes four equal destination blocks immediately after an already detailed masthead.
- Evidence and Industrial Proof have important boundaries, but they are surrounded by many visually comparable panels.
- Lab Note details are readable, yet the evidence rail and repeated ending dominate short articles.
- Footer group density is excessive at 390px despite correct semantic order.

### Required correction

Prefer open editorial sequences and rows for discovery/reference content, maintain one dominant module per route, and compress shared endings without hiding or removing destinations.

The conceptual models now expose their three-step information hierarchy without nested cards. Recovery links on 404 read as an ordered utility sequence.
