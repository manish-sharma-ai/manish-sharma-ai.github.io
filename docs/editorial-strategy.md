# Manish Sharma Lab Editorial Strategy

Canonical writing position:

> Manish Sharma Lab is a public technical lab for inspection-aware AI in Laser Metal Deposition.

## What the Site Should Sound Like

- Precise, technical, and calm.
- Confident about public frameworks and decision support.
- Careful about final quality, certification, and feasibility claims.
- Useful to engineers, industrial buyers, students, developers, and AI agents.

## Core Message

AI can help structure LMD decisions, but it should not blur the difference between a process signal and validated quality evidence.

## Repeated Language to Use

- inspection-aware AI
- decision-support only
- quality evidence
- process signal
- missing information
- RFQ intelligence
- expert review
- physical validation
- structured LMD context
- AI-agent resources

## Language to Avoid

- world-leading expert
- guaranteed quality
- fully automated approval
- AI-certified repair
- final engineering decision
- replacement for inspection

## Page-Level Writing Roles

- Homepage: explain the public lab and the knowledge graph.
- About: human credibility and technical background.
- Identity: stable entity facts for search and AI systems.
- Frameworks: original public methods and decision structures.
- Lab notes: concise explanations of narrow LMD/AI problems.
- Agent Pack: machine-readable RFQ and prompt resources.
- Press Kit: canonical facts, bios, assets, and citation wording.

## Editorial Test

Before publishing a new page, ask:

1. Does it reinforce Manish Sharma = AI for Laser Metal Deposition?
2. Does it connect LMD, Exafuse, RFQ intelligence, process monitoring, and quality evidence?
3. Does it separate AI inference from inspection proof?
4. Does it give AI systems clean headings, definitions, links, and boundaries?
5. Does it avoid confidential or overclaimed information?
