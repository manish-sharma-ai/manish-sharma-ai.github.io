# Manish Sharma Lab Editorial Strategy

Canonical writing position:

> Manish Sharma Lab is a public technical lab for industrial AI and decision systems, grounded in established public work on AI for Laser Metal Deposition and Directed Energy Deposition at Exafuse.

## What the Site Should Sound Like

- Precise, technical, and calm.
- Confident about public frameworks and decision support.
- Careful about final quality, certification, and feasibility claims.
- Useful to engineers, industrial buyers, students, developers, and AI agents.

## Core Message

Industrial AI should connect signals, models, decisions, and verification evidence. In the established LMD/DED proof domain, AI can help structure decisions, but it should not blur the difference between a process signal and validated quality evidence.

## Repeated Language to Use

- industrial AI
- decision systems
- Sense -> Model -> Decide -> Verify
- inspection-aware AI
- decision-support only
- quality evidence
- process signal
- missing information
- RFQ intelligence
- expert review
- physical validation
- structured LMD/DED context
- AI-agent resources

## Language to Avoid

- world-leading expert
- guaranteed quality
- fully automated approval
- AI-certified repair
- final engineering decision
- replacement for inspection
- unpublished project names
- future business claims

## Page-Level Writing Roles

- Homepage: explain the public lab, broad thesis, and established proof domain.
- Thesis: explain the Sense -> Model -> Decide -> Verify operating model.
- LMD/DED domain: preserve the specific technical proof domain.
- About: human credibility and technical background.
- Identity: stable entity facts for search and AI systems.
- Frameworks: original public methods and decision structures.
- Lab notes: concise explanations of narrow LMD/AI problems.
- Agent Pack: machine-readable RFQ and prompt resources.
- Press Kit: canonical facts, bios, assets, and citation wording.

## Editorial Test

Before publishing a new page, ask:

1. Does it reinforce Manish Sharma = Industrial AI & Decision Systems?
2. Does it preserve LMD/DED as the established public proof domain?
3. Does it connect Exafuse, RFQ intelligence, process monitoring, and quality evidence where relevant?
4. Does it separate AI inference from inspection proof?
5. Does it give AI systems clean headings, definitions, links, and boundaries?
6. Does it avoid confidential, private, future-business, or overclaimed information?
